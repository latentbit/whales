Whales

Whales are giant marine mammals that live in oceans all around the world. Even though they look like fish, they actually breath air, are warm-blooded, and feed milk to their babies - just like humans and other mammals.

1. Introduction

Whales are some of the largest animals on Earth. They evolved from land mammals millions of years ago and later adapted to life in the ocean. The biggest animal ever known is the Blue Whale.

2. Habitat

Whales live in oceans worldwide:

  • Arctic Ocean
  • Pacific Ocean
  • Atlantic Ocean
  • Indian Ocean

Some whales prefer cold waters, while others migrate thousands of kilometers to warm tropical waters to give birth.

3. Characteristics

Common whale traits:

  • Breath through blowholes on top of their heads
  • Warm-blooded mammals
  • Very intelligent and social
  • Use sounds and songs to communicate
  • Have thick fat layers called blubber to stay warm
  • Some species are enormous - longer than buses

4. Different Types of Whales

  • Blue Whale - the biggest
  • Humpback Whale - known for songs and jumping
  • Sperm Whale - deep diver with a huge head
  • Orca - black-and-white hunters
  • Beluga Whale - white whale with expressive sounds
  • Narwhal - the unicorn of the sea
